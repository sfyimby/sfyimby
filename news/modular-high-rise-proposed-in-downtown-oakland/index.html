Modular High-Rise Proposed in Downtown Oakland

Diego Aguilar Canabal
Tuesday, October 18, 2016

Oakland-based development firm Nautilus Group will soon undergo the challenge of building the country’s second tallest modular apartment complex, a 350-foot building with 176 dwelling units at the 14th & Webster intersection in downtown Oakland. The mixed-use project, featuring office space and ground-floor retail typical of downtown developments, will receive a 20% density bonus for including on-site affordable housing, with rents price-indexed for middle- and/or low-income households.

Although renderings have already been released, the developer likely faces a long road ahead. A 32-story modular high-rise project at Atlantic Yards in Brooklyn has faced a litany of problems since its inception, including but not limited to competing lawsuits between the developer and contractor. Stalled for years, the B2 tower at Atlantic Yards is finally complete.

1433 Webster, current street view (source: Google Maps / OakBuilt)

Elsewhere in the world, there is more optimism regarding modular construction. For example, a 54-story tower was built in Chengshe, China in just 19 days.

RAD Urban, a partner in Nautilus’s vertically-integrated business model, released a promotional video earlier this year boasting of “up to 50%” speedier construction with their pre-fabricated modular designs. Nautilus recently completed “Garden Village,” a much smaller project at the Dwight & Fulton intersection in Berkeley, featuring a rooftop garden and a total absence of private parking spots.

The project will be heard before Oakland’s Design Review Committee, a subcommittee of the Planning Commission, on October 26th.

A staff report from the City of Oakland is expected later in the week. This article will be updated accordingly.
